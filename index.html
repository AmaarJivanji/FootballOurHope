Football Our Hope

The Street is Our Home. The Home of the Barefoot Children

Community and Service. Aga Khan Academy Mombasa.

What is the significance and vision of Football Our Hope?

Streetchildren are amongst the most physically visible of all children on the planet; living and working on streets in cities all over the world. However, stigmatizing and limiting societal notions by mainstream society force street children to be arguably the most invisible and vulnerable children of our time.

Bridge

Street children build a sense of belonging and identity, that is fostered through recreational activities and interaction between people of multiple backgrounds, thus promoting pluralism.

Activity

Service group carries out weekly sports related activities such as swimming and football. Training and equipment is provided by the group members who also participate and engage with the streetchildren through the playing of sports. Sport activities have been commended to increase the general happiness of the children, alongside the various health benefits they gain.

Support

Fund-raising events, talent display, sales and donations has helped raise money to support children to attain basic needs and services, including job creation.

Sustain

Streetchildren manage to fight off isolation and divert their mindsets from vices such as theft, violent extremism and theft. FOH is not just a charitable organization but, one that also teaches important life skills and works to develop strong, postive mindsets.
